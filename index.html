DAY 02 TUE 28 JUL

From Climate Concepts to Field Monitoring

08:00 – 21:30
— 08:00–09:00 Breakfast Complex Măgura, Buzău
01 Introduction and Context 09:00–10:00
Speaker: Dragoș Tătaru — Senior Researcher, National Institute for Earth Physics (NIEP)

An opening session introducing the EASE project, its objectives, and the role of the Romanian Summer School as a learning and testing event. Covers the structure of the week, expected outcomes and how teacher training, educational resources and monitoring instruments connect to pilot-school implementation.

Participants will be able toUnderstand the project's purpose and structure, see how the sessions connect, and recognise their role in testing and later transferring EASE resources to their own schools.
Complex Măgura, Buzău
— 10:00–10:15 Coffee break Complex Măgura, Buzău
02 Measuring a Changing Climate 10:15–12:00
Speakers: Bogdan Antonescu & Răzvan Pîrloagă — Faculty of Physics, University of Bucharest

The scientific grounding for the week's practical work: the difference between weather and climate, how observation has evolved from historical records to modern instrumental datasets, and the core atmospheric parameters — temperature, pressure, humidity, precipitation, wind, solar radiation.

Participants will be able toDistinguish weather variability from climate trends, identify key monitoring parameters, and explain why data quality, continuity and comparability matter for reliable climate interpretation.
Complex Măgura, Buzău
— 12:00–13:30 Lunch Complex Măgura, Buzău
03 "Dissecting" the Monitoring Instruments 13:30–16:00
Trainers: Dr Eduard Năstase, Dr Mihai Boni & Alexandru Macovei — NIEP

Hands-on exploration of the instruments used throughout the week — weather stations, environmental sensors, educational seismometers and spectrometry-related devices — examined as modular, open systems. Strong emphasis on 3D printing and digital fabrication for building low-cost DIY instruments, plus good practice for station siting and installation.

Participants will be able toIdentify each instrument's components and function, weigh the pros and cons of DIY educational instruments, and spot common installation errors before setting up a school station.
Camping Pâclele Mici (la Hangar) / Mud Volcanoes
04 Monitoring Instruments in the Field: Pâclele Mici Mud Volcanoes 17:00–20:00
Trainers: Dragoș Tătaru, Dr Eduard Năstase, Dr Mihai Boni & Alexandru Macovei — NIEP

A field visit to a real monitoring site at the Pâclele Mici Mud Volcanoes, observing professional and educational meteorological, seismic and GNSS equipment in place. Discussion of how exposure, topography and local conditions shape data quality, with access to selected datasets recorded on site.

Participants will be able toRecognise field-monitoring equipment in situ, judge whether a site is suitable for a given measurement, and connect local topography and surface conditions to microclimate and data bias.
Camping Pâclele Mici (la Hangar) / Mud Volcanoes
— 20:30–21:30 Dinner Camping Pâclele Mici (la Hangar) / Mud Volcanoes
DAY 03 WED 29 JUL

From Scientific Instruments to Classroom & Field Inquiry

08:00 – 21:00
— 08:00–09:00 Breakfast Complex Măgura, Buzău
01 From Scientific Concepts to Classroom Activities 09:00–10:00
Instructor: Joshua Biro — Teacher

Turning scientific instruments into meaningful classroom learning. Explores how a weather station, rain gauge, seismometer or sensor can anchor an inquiry activity — defining a clear question, choosing achievable outcomes, using low-cost materials, and designing a genuine final product for students.

Session outputEach participant sketches a short classroom activity: instrument or concept, one inquiry question, data to collect, student task, final product, and one limitation to discuss.
Complex Măgura, Buzău
02 Parallel Hands-On Workshops — session 1 10:00–11:00
Trainers: Dr Eduard Năstase, Dr Mihai Boni & Alexandru Macovei — NIEP

First rotation of two parallel workshops built around the workflow build or examine → measure → observe → analyse → interpret → communicate, each combining a main hands-on activity with shorter demonstration stations.

Workshop 1 — Spectrometry & meteorologySTEAM activities: build or examine a low-cost 3D-printed spectrometer, read spectra from different light sources, link optics to solar radiation and atmospheric monitoring.
Workshop 2 — SeismologySTEAM activities (activity 1 from the resource guide): assemble a DIY seismometer, generate controlled vibrations and separate signal from local noise.
Complex Măgura, Buzău
— 11:00–11:10 Coffee break Complex Măgura, Buzău
03 Parallel Hands-On Workshops — session 2 11:10–12:00
Trainers: Dr Eduard Năstase, Dr Mihai Boni & Alexandru Macovei — NIEP

Second rotation: groups switch workshops so every participant experiences both Spectrometry & meteorology and Seismology STEAM activities.

Complex Măgura, Buzău
— 12:00–13:30 Lunch Complex Măgura, Buzău
04 Field Learning Circuit: Reading Environmental & Geological Change 14:00–18:00
Trainers: EASE Summer School scientific and educational team

Earth Learning Ideas on the ground — a guided circuit stopping at sites chosen for their geological and environmental relevance. At each stop, short observations and measurements connect present-day evidence — erosion, vegetation, microclimate — to the landscape's geological and climatic history.

Participants will be able toRun simple field-observation protocols, compare conditions across sites, and design a short, transferable field activity for their own students.
Field site near Ulmet (location to be confirmed)

Field Kit

What to bring

Part classroom, part field trip — pack for both.

As specified by the organisers

  • Comfortable clothes for both indoor sessions and outdoor fieldwork
  • Sturdy field shoes — the programme includes site visits to the mud volcanoes and a geology field circuit
  • Laptop, if possible

Good to pack

  • Sun protection — hat, sunscreen — for the outdoor field sessions
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Notebook & pen for the field circuit and workshop notes
  • Universal power adapter, if travelling from outside Romania's plug type
  • ID / travel documents for airport transfer

General travel suggestions in addition to the organisers' list above.
